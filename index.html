Fundamental Articles

Article 1

Freedom & Equality

"All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights."

Article 4

No Slavery

"No one shall be held in slavery or servitude; slavery is prohibited in all forms."

Article 26

Right to Education

"Everyone has the right to education. Education shall be free."

Key Milestones

539 B.C.

Cyrus Cylinder

Cyrus the Great conquers Babylon and frees the slaves, declaring all people have the right to choose their own religion.

1215

Magna Carta

Signed in England, it established that everyone, including the king, was subject to the law.

1945

Creation of the UN

50 nations met in San Francisco to form the United Nations to promote peace.

1948

Universal Declaration

The UDHR is adopted by the UN General Assembly in Paris. 30 articles for all humanity.
